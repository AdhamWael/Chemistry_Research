Q3: What is The Law of Conservation of Mass?

The Law: Mass is neither created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction, but it can be transformed from one form to another.

Balanced equations support this law by showing there is an equal number of each type of atom on both sides of the equation.

Q4: What happens to atoms during a reaction?

  • ‚Ä¢ Atoms are not created or destroyed.
  • ‚Ä¢ Bonds between atoms break.
  • ‚Ä¢ New bonds form.
  • ‚Ä¢ Atoms are simply rearranged to make new substances.

In simple words: Atoms change partners, but none disappear or appear.

What is a catalyst and does it get used up in a reaction?

KINETIC_CORE // ANALYZER_ID.404

A catalyst is a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being changed or consumed.

It helps the reaction happen faster by lowering the activation energy and is regenerated at the end.

A catalyst does not get used up in the reaction.

Balancing chemical equations is essential because it ensures the law of conservation of mass is followed, meaning the number of atoms of each element is the same before and after the reaction. This scientific accuracy allows chemists to predict how much of each substance is needed or produced. In real-world applications, such as medicine, industry, and laboratory experiments, balanced equations help avoid waste, ensure safety, and achieve the desired results efficiently.
